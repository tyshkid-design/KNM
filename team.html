Managing Partner

H.M. Muriuki (Mrs)

Hellen is an Advocate of the High Court of Kenya, having been admitted to the Bar in 1998, and a Certified Public Secretary. Her experience spans both private legal practice and in-house legal roles, as well as board secretarial work with major institutions.

Hellen holds a Bachelor's Degree in Law (LLB) from the University of Nairobi, a Masters in Corporate Law (LLM) from the University of South Africa (UNISA), and a Diploma from the Kenya School of Law. She has also undertaken specialised training in Human Resources.

As Managing Partner, Hellen oversees the day-to-day management of the firm while maintaining an active practice. She represents clients in court and advises on insurance and commercial transactions, company law, competition law, constitutional law, transaction advisory, and intellectual property — bringing depth, breadth, and strategic clarity to every matter she handles.

Her work with major financial institutions has encompassed due diligence exercises, securitisation transactions, preparation and registration of transfers, charges, mortgages, and chattel transfers. She has also led governance audit engagements and provided board secretarial support at the highest levels.

Partner – Litigation

Christine Kawira Ungu

Christine is an Advocate of the High Court of Kenya, having been admitted to the Bar in 1999, and a Certified Public Secretary. Her professional journey encompasses private legal practice, in-house corporate legal work, and experience from the bench — giving her a uniquely well-rounded perspective on dispute resolution.

Christine holds a Bachelor of Laws (LLB) degree from the University of Nairobi and a Diploma from the Kenya School of Law. She is currently pursuing a Master of Business Administration in Strategy Management at Kenya Methodist University, deepening her commercial and strategic advisory capabilities.

As the partner in charge of litigation at J.N & Co Advocates, Christine leads the firm's contentious practice, representing clients in civil, commercial, constitutional, and land matters before the courts in Kiambu County, Nairobi, and beyond. She is known for her meticulous preparation, courtroom composure, and ability to achieve practical, client-centred outcomes.

Christine has also successfully represented clients in alternative dispute resolution (ADR) proceedings — including arbitration and mediation — securing favourable outcomes efficiently and cost-effectively.
