FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to questions our new clients commonly ask before their first consultation.

How do I book an appointment?

You can book using the form on this page, call us on +254 722 818932, email us at info@jnnadvocates.co.ke, or send us a WhatsApp message. We offer in-office, phone, and video consultations.

What should I bring to my first consultation?

Bring any relevant documents, correspondence, contracts, or evidence related to your matter. Identification (national ID or passport) is also helpful. The more context you provide, the more effectively we can advise you.

Do you offer free initial consultations?

Please contact our offices directly to enquire about consultation fees for your specific type of matter. We aim to be transparent about costs from the outset so you can make an informed decision.

Which areas do you serve?

We serve clients across Kenya, with a particular focus on Kiambu County, Nairobi, Runda, Thindigua, Kasarani, Ridgeways, Muthaiga, Westlands, and Nairobi CBD. We can also assist clients remotely.

Can you handle urgent or emergency legal matters?

Yes. If you have an urgent matter requiring an injunction, urgent court application, or immediate legal advice, please call us directly on +254 722 818932 rather than emailing, and we will prioritise your matter.

How long does a typical legal matter take?

Timelines vary depending on the nature and complexity of the matter — from a few days for a straightforward conveyancing transaction to several months for complex litigation. We will give you a realistic estimate during your consultation.

Is my consultation confidential?

Absolutely. All communications between you and J.N & Co Advocates are protected by legal professional privilege and treated with the utmost confidentiality. We take client privacy extremely seriously.

Can you assist with matters outside Kenya?

Our practice is focused on Kenyan law. However, we have professional networks and can refer clients to trusted counterpart firms for matters requiring expertise in other jurisdictions where appropriate.
